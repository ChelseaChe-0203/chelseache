Chelsea Tianyi Che

PhD Student in Behavioral Decision Making

UCLA Anderson School of Management

My research explores how motivation, incentives, and behavioral interventions shape behavior and decision-making in organizations and other real-world settings. I use both field and lab experiments to study these questions.

My current work includes field experiments on public service payments and preventive health behavior, as well as projects on gender gaps in academic labor markets, with a particular focus on faculty hiring and pay.

Before joining UCLA Anderson, I received my bachelor’s degree in economics from Peking University.
